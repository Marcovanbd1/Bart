The cognitive architecture of the consultation room

The problem runs deeper than memory. It is metacognition under emotional load.

When a patient sits in front of a clinician who uses the word cancer, or dementia, or complicated, something happens to cognition that memory alone cannot explain. Metacognitive capacity collapses. The patient cannot monitor their own comprehension. They leave the room with fragments, and they have no way of knowing which fragments are missing. Health literacy and memory are downstream of it. The information was given. The capacity to receive it was temporarily unavailable.

Accurate information that cannot be received in the moment it arrives is not the same as useful information.

Emotional Architecture asks what this specific patient, in this specific emotional state, with this specific relational context, can actually receive and use. That question sits before the summary is generated. It shapes every word.

The methodology

Five dimensions. Every clinical encounter. Every summary.

Emotional Architecture maps the emotional climate of a clinical encounter across five dimensions. Each one is a clinical parameter that determines whether post-consultation information becomes action or remains noise.

Tone Gradient
The emotional register of the consultation and how it should travel into the summary. A cancer diagnosis conversation and a routine medication review require fundamentally different tonal calibration, not just different content.
Disclosure Readiness
What the patient was ready to hear in the room, and what they are ready to receive when the summary arrives. The summary reaches the patient at a different point in their processing of what happened. The two moments require different calibration.
Omission Topology
What should be left out, and why. Every omission is a decision. Examination findings that would worry without purpose. Diagnoses the patient already carries. Information that belongs to the clinical record but not to the patient's next twenty-four hours.
Relational Load
Who else is carrying this information with the patient. A partner. A daughter. A carer. A social worker. The summary is never read by one person in isolation. Relational Load determines how the information should be structured for the people who will receive it together.
Temporal Pressure
What needs to happen, and by when. The patient travelling on Sunday. The medication that starts tonight. The decision that has to be made before Friday. Temporal Pressure determines the hierarchy of action in the summary.
Where this lives in Ditto

EA doesn't sit after the summary. It sits before it.

Emotional Architecture is not a quality check applied after the LLM has produced something. It is the framework that determines what the LLM is instructed to do before a word of the summary is generated. The five dimensions run as parameters at the configuration layer. The summary that comes out the other end is shaped by those parameters from the first word.

Your medical team currently sets the rules for the summaries and red-teams the output. That is exactly the right instinct. What CoLab brings is the formal methodology that makes those rules clinically defensible, philosophically grounded, and consistently applied across every consultation type, every language, and every market Ditto enters.

The Dutch medical team sets rules based on Dutch clinical norms. EA sets rules based on how human beings receive difficult information under emotional load. That doesn't change at a border.

An informal proposal

What a partnership between CoLab and Ditto could look like.

This is not a vendor relationship. CoLab is not offering to build something for Ditto. The proposal is a partnership between two organisations that are building toward the same insight from different directions. Ditto has the product, the scale, and the consumer reach. CoLab has the methodology, the clinical governance framework, and the frontline NHS expertise that makes the product defensible in every regulated healthcare system it enters.

The shape of it
Neighbourhood CoLab CIC as Ditto's global clinical methodology partner.
  • EA is embedded at Ditto's summary configuration layer globally, not as a UK-specific add-on but as the foundational methodology that shapes every summary in every market.
  • CoLab works alongside Ditto's medical team to configure EA for the three life stage anchors: birth, cancer, and cognitive decline. These are the consultations where the emotional climate matters most and where the clinical consequences of getting it wrong are most serious.
  • As Ditto enters each new market, CoLab configures EA for the local clinical context, cultural norms around disclosure, and regulatory framework. The methodology is market-agnostic. Its application is market-specific.
  • The partnership generates real-world evidence for EA at scale. Tens of thousands of EA-shaped summaries across multiple markets and consultation types. That evidence is jointly owned and jointly publishable. It is the clinical validation that makes both Ditto and CoLab more credible in every conversation that follows.
  • The commercial structure is a CoLab CIC licence agreement: a modest monthly retainer reflecting CoLab's ongoing methodology work, with a royalty structure tied to summary volume at scale. The retainer acknowledges the work. The royalty acknowledges the value.
